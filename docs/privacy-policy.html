Privacy Policy - Prevent Duplicate Tabs

Last updated: February 2025

Overview

Prevent Duplicate Tabs is a Chrome extension that helps you avoid opening duplicate browser tabs. This policy describes what data the extension uses and where it is stored.

Data We Use

  • Browser tabs – The extension uses the tabs permission to read tab URLs only for duplicate detection. It does not read page content, and it does not store or send your browsing history anywhere.
  • Your settings – Preferences you set in the extension (e.g. duplicate action, exception URLs/domains, site-specific rules) are stored locally on your device using Chrome’s storage API.
  • Statistics – A single count of how many duplicate tabs the extension has closed is stored locally. No other usage or analytics data is collected.

Where Data Is Stored

All data is stored in Chrome’s sync storage:

  • Chrome sync storage – Your settings and the tabs-closed count are saved in Chrome’s sync storage and are synced across your signed-in Chrome profile. We do not send this data to our own servers.

We do not send your data to any external servers. No analytics, tracking, or third-party services are used.

Permissions

  • tabs – Used to read tab URLs for duplicate detection and to close or switch tabs when a duplicate is found.
  • storage – Used to save your settings and the tabs-closed count in Chrome sync storage.
