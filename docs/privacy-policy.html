Privacy Policy - Prevent Duplicate Tabs

Last updated: May 2026

Overview

Prevent Duplicate Tabs is a Chrome extension that helps you avoid opening duplicate browser tabs. You can allow duplicates for specific sites, override behavior per domain, or open a single tab without prevention by right-clicking a link. This policy describes what data the extension uses and where it is stored.

Data We Use

  • Browser tabs – The extension uses the tabs permission to read tab URLs only for duplicate detection and to close, switch, or open tabs as part of your settings. It does not read page content, and it does not store or send your browsing history anywhere.
  • Your settings – Preferences you set in the extension (e.g. duplicate action, scope for duplicate check (all windows or current window only), exception URLs/domains, site-specific rules) are stored locally on your device using Chrome’s storage API.
  • Statistics – A single count of how many duplicate tabs the extension has closed is stored locally. No other usage or analytics data is collected.
  • Review prompt – If shown, whether you dismissed or snoozed the optional “rate the extension” reminder is stored locally only (chrome.storage.local). No account or identity is involved.

Where Data Is Stored

All extension data stays on your device through Chrome’s storage APIs:

  • Chrome sync storage – Your settings and the tabs-closed count are saved in Chrome’s sync storage and may sync across your signed-in Chrome profile. We do not send this data to our own servers.
  • Chrome local storage – Review-prompt timing (dismissed/snoozed) is stored locally only and is not synced.

We do not send your settings, tab URLs, or browsing history to our own servers. No analytics, tracking, or third-party advertising services are used.

Third-Party Services

The extension popup and options page load the Poppins font from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com). When you open those pages, your browser may request font files from Google under Google’s privacy policy. The extension does not send your settings or tab data to Google.

Permissions

  • tabs – Read tab URLs for duplicate detection; close or switch tabs when a duplicate is found; open a tab when you use “Open in new tab (allow duplicate).”
  • storage – Save your settings, statistics, and review-prompt state on your device.
  • windows – Focus the correct window when switching to a tab in another window and close empty windows after a duplicate tab is removed. No window data is stored or sent elsewhere.
  • contextMenus – Show “Open in new tab (allow duplicate)” when you right-click a normal web link (<a href>). Chrome provides the link URL to the extension only for that menu action; the extension does not inject scripts into web pages.

The extension does not request broad host permissions and does not run content scripts on websites you visit.
